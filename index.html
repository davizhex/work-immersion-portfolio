    My journey and Skills Development

    As I reflect upon my transformative journey at St. Francis Caracciolo Culinary Academy as a TVL Senior High School student, I am struck by the profound impact this experience has had on my personal growth, my culinary skills, and my understanding of the world. The path that lead me to discover the depths of my passion, and the strength of my character.

    My journey began as I enrolled at this school as a grade 11 student, it was the first time experiencing this school and I was already met by warm and embracing greetings by the students and the cheerful, creative and talented teachers. And I would like to thank my former teacher Ma'am Audrey for the warm and memorable greetings, how could I ever forget the how lovely and cheerful she was, like a sunshine guiding you to a blissful journey. It was because of her I made some things possible and to my former teacher sir Kenny who never stop to believed in me, who never stopped to give up on me even I've already given up to myself, and how could I ever forget about the times where he offered me lunch as I refuse to eat because of consciousness in gaining weight. Those moments are the ones that truly mattered and will forever carry with me as it shaped me and nurtured me as a person.

    The Pastry and Bread Making NC ll was more than just an introduction to the art of baking; it was a immersion into a world of precision, patience, and patience. Under the guiding hands of our esteemed chefs, we delved into the science and craft of creating the perfect bread and pastry, and the artistic cakes that would made our chef proud. From the initial mixing and kneading to the final glazing and decorating, each step was a lesson in attention to detail, a testament to the notion that perfection is achieved through dedication. The hours spent in the kitchen, our hands dusted with flour and our minds focused on the task at hand, taught us the value of hard work and the rewards that come with perseverance.

    As we progressed through the curriculum, the Food and Beverage Service (FBS) training and the final cookery course, I began to understand that the art of cooking was about more than just the end result on the plate. It was about the entire dining experience. The FBS training was a eye-opening glimpse into the world of hospitality, the importance of gracious service, and the delicate balance between the needs of the guest. We learned to think on our feet, and to maintain a composure under the pressure.

    Throughout my time at SFCCA, I discovered that the true essence of the culinary arts lies not just in the technical proficiency, but in the relationships we build and the character.
